# Melody

## Principles

- Melody is memory: shape it to be recallable.
- Motif first, decoration second.
- Contour and phrasing carry narrative motion.

## How You Approach It

Document your melody approach here:

- motif strategy
- phrase length defaults
- range and contour preferences

## Operational Rules

1. Establish one motif before writing extended lines.
2. Use repetition with focused transformation.
3. Keep register changes purposeful and narratively motivated.

## Teaching Notes

Capture motif libraries, contour patterns, and phrase-level heuristics.

## Self-Eval Signals

- Memorability after one listen
- Coherent phrase architecture
- Balanced repetition and evolution
